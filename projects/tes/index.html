Branding Project

Project Name Technical for Engineering Services
Project Type Branding
Project Date March, 2025
Project Location Alexandria, Egypt

OVERVIEW

Technical is an engineering services company that has specialized over the past years in steel construction works and providing cleanroom solutions for pharmaceutical and cosmetics factories. Building on this remarkable track record, the company established a specialized factory for manufacturing steel used in electromechanical contracting and sterile room doors.

MASTER BRANDMARK

Primary Version

The logo fundamentally represents the core essence of Technical’s work, which is transforming raw materials into functional objects. The logo embodies strength, reflecting the essential quality needed in this field to shape conditions into functional spaces.

The T.E.S logo mark features bold, geometric shapes symbolizing strength and stability, reflecting the engineering and construction industry. The dark blue conveys trust and professionalism, while the golden yellow signifies quality and innovation. The overlapping squares and rectangles hint at structure and assembly, reinforcing the brand’s technical expertise. The T.E.S logotype uses a clean, bold sans-serif font with uppercase letters, conveying confidence and authority. The spacing ensures clarity, while the dots between letters emphasize precision. The tagline, "Technical for Engineering Services," adds a formal, professional touch.

Brand Applications

Below are some of possible brand applications that can be used

Business Cards

Business cards follow a strict format, designedto make a customer’s first face-to-face contact with a Technical representative consistent across departments.

Letterhead

Stationery follows a standard format, positioning our logo top left (within the margin) and the address at the base of the format. Electronic artwork has been provided for producing A4 company letterheads. Never attempt to redraw or recreate the artwork.

Compliment Slips

Compliment slips follow the same principles as those specified for letterheads – positioning our logo top left (within the margin) and theaddress at the base of the format. Electronic artwork has been provided for producing compliment slips. Never attempt to redraw orrecreate the artwork.

Memo

The example opposite demonstrates the memo template. Unlike the fax template, the memo template – intended for internal use – does not carry the address. To ensure ease of use – standard system fonts have been specified for setting text – . The base template has been designed to ensure consistency of application of the brand assets and should not be altered in any manner. Electronic templates have been created for both Arabic and English memos. Never attempt to redrawor recreate the templates provided.

Envelopes

Envelopes use our logo and other information in much the same wayas other stationery. Electronic artwork has been provided for producing DL, C5, C4 and C3 envelopes. Never attempt to redraw or recreate the artwork.

Folder

Folders, while slightly larger than the A4 documents they contain, have been developed to convey a sense of simplicity and confidence making use of a heavier stock and special finishes. Front covers carry the pattern. For contrast purposes and to highlight internal documentation, the inside prints full bleed in Blue and incorporates two ‘pockets’. Die-cuts have further been included toaccommodate a business card. Electronic artwork has been created for producing A4 folders. Never attempt to redraw or recreate the artwork provided.

Certificates

The example opposite demonstrates the effective use of the brand identity components on A4 Certificate 210 × 297mm. These guidelines therefore include a layout and specifications for both Arabic and English use. Never attempt to redraw or recreate the template provided .

Employee ID

Employee badges and lanyards are designed to emphasize the brand elements by using the logo elements as strokes and borders for the whole design. Electronic artwork has been created for employee badge. Never attempt to redraw or recreate the artwork provided.

Social Media

The opposite example illustrates the use of identity in designing a banner image for various social media sites.
